 
 Personara.ai / TeamSync Executive Prompt Library 
 Codex-ready premium starter prompts for CEOs, senior executives, CHROs, and HR leaders, including the Boardroom Scenarios add-on. 
 Document purpose 
 A productized library of starter prompts for premium executive customers using loaded Gallup Strengths data inside TeamSync. 
 Primary users 
 CEO, executive team members, CHRO, HR leaders, strategy leaders, founders, board-facing management teams. 
 Product tiers 
 Premium Executive Library; Boardroom Scenarios add-on. 
 Codex intent 
 Each prompt includes a stable ID, tier, category, title, canonical prompt text, and fixed response contract for app wiring. 
 How to use this library 
 • Assume all relevant executive Gallup Strengths profiles have already been loaded into TeamSync. 
 • Prompts should drive strengths-aware analysis, not generic leadership commentary. 
 • Outputs should identify likely behavior, strengths advantages, blind spots, friction patterns, role-fit, and practical action steps. 
 • The Boardroom Scenarios pack is positioned as an additional paid layer above Premium, designed for governance, investor, capital, and board-pressure simulations. 
 Core analysis instruction for the prompt engine 
 Use the loaded Gallup Strengths of each executive to assess likely behavior, contribution patterns, friction points, blind spots, decision risks, leadership fit, and capability gaps. Give practical recommendations, not generic personality commentary. Ground the answer in the actual strengths profile of the team. 
 Standard response contract 
 Output section 
 Required content 
 1. Situation read 
 Brief summary of the challenge or decision context. 
 2. Likely team behavior 
 How the executive team is likely to respond based on strengths. 
 3. Strengths advantages 
 What the team is naturally well positioned to do. 
 4. Blind spots and risks 
 Where the team may misread, delay, overreact, or collide. 
 5. Key tension points 
 Likely executive-to-executive friction or role confusion. 
 6. Recommended actions 
 Practical next steps, role moves, decision structure, or routines. 
 7. Capability gaps 
 What is missing, overloaded, or should be added or borrowed.
