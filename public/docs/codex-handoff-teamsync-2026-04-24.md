# TeamSync Deep Research Brief
## CEO and Board Requirements, Scenario Testing, Gallup Integration, and Practitioner Growth

Date: April 24, 2026
Prepared for: PersonaFoundry / TeamSync

## 1) Executive Summary

TeamSync can be positioned as an organizational resilience and governance intelligence layer, not just an executive team dynamics tool. The strongest strategic wedge is to combine:
- Gallup engagement and strengths science
- Scenario simulation across workforce, operating, cyber, and market shocks
- Board-ready governance reporting with clear risk appetite, tolerance, and action tracking

Core thesis: TeamSync should turn people and operating signals into pre-decision intelligence for CEOs and boards, then translate each simulation outcome into practical role, capability, and operating-rhythm actions.

## 2) What CEOs and Boards Need from TeamSync

Based on current governance and disclosure direction, boards expect management to demonstrate disciplined oversight of strategy execution, risk, and resilience.

### 2.1 Board-Level Requirements

1. Strategic guidance plus risk oversight in one system
- Boards need strategy and risk treated as one decision loop, not separate conversations.
- OECD Principles (Sept 11, 2023) emphasize board responsibility for strategic guidance, monitoring management, and oversight of risk systems.

2. Explicit risk appetite and tolerance
- Leaders need defined tolerances per critical operation and named risk owners.
- APRA CPS 230 (effective July 1, 2025) requires critical operation tolerances and testing against severe but plausible scenarios.

3. Scenario-tested resilience
- Boards increasingly need evidence that leadership teams can respond to severe-but-plausible disruptions.
- IFRS S2 (effective Jan 1, 2024) requires climate resilience assessment informed by scenario analysis.

4. Governance-quality disclosure readiness
- Public companies must support cyber governance and incident disclosure workflows.
- SEC final cyber rules (July 26, 2023) require disclosure of material cyber incidents and annual disclosure of cyber risk management, strategy, and governance.

5. Execution quality under volatility
- WEF Global Risks Report 2026 (published Jan 14, 2026) highlights multi-horizon risk pressure (2026, to 2028, to 2036).

### 2.2 CEO-Level Requirements

CEOs need TeamSync to answer:
- Do we have the right leadership architecture for the strategy horizon we are entering?
- Which executive-team failure modes are most likely under pressure?
- What operating rhythm will improve decision quality without slowing execution?
- Where are our hidden capability gaps and succession fragilities?

## 3) Gallup Research Signals that Matter for TeamSync

## 3.1 Engagement and outcomes
- Gallup Q12 Meta-Analysis (11th edition, published May 2024, updated July 2024) reports meaningful differences between top- and bottom-engagement groups, including:
  - 23% profitability
  - 18% productivity (sales)
  - 78% absenteeism
  - 63% safety incidents
  - 28% shrinkage

Implication for TeamSync: engagement should be modeled as a leading operational indicator, not a lagging HR metric.

## 3.2 Manager leverage
- Gallup reports managers account for 70% of the variance in team engagement.
- Updated Gallup manager development guidance (Feb 2026) cites that coaching/people-development training can increase team engagement (up to 18%) and improve manager performance metrics (20% to 28%).

Implication: TeamSync should prioritize manager-system design and coaching cadence as central resilience controls.

## 3.3 Current workplace volatility
- State of the Global Workplace 2026 reports global engagement declined to 20% in 2025 and manager engagement declined notably (31% in 2022 to 22% in 2025).

Implication: TeamSync scenarios should treat manager capability and load as a first-order risk driver.

## 3.4 Strengths and commercial outcomes
- Gallup strengths meta-analysis references data from more than 1 million individuals and almost 50,000 business units.
- Gallup 2026 award reporting cites strengths-oriented cultures associated with:
  - 23% higher engagement
  - 29% higher profit
  - 19% higher sales
  - 72% lower turnover

Implication: TeamSync should convert strengths data from descriptive profiles into decision-role fit and execution design.

## 3.5 AI adoption reality
- Gallup (Apr 13, 2026) reports frequent AI use growth (13% daily, 28% frequent usage among U.S. employees).

Implication: TeamSync should include AI adoption maturity and manager enablement as scenario variables in operating resilience.
